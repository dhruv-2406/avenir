A Productive Lockdown

When fishermen don’t go to sea, they repair their nets’.
– Nabil Sabio Azadi.
Life as we knew changed on midnight of 24th March when Prime Minister
Mr.Narendra Modi announced a lockdown of 21 days to
prevent the spread of COVID19 throughout the country. Despite of
enormous attempts by the government, the virus travelled quickly
to various parts of the country and Prime Minister had no other
choice than extending it till 3rd May. Everyone except those
working in Healthcare sector and food production were not
allowed to leave their homes.

This seemed as a wonderful opportunity to binge watch our favorite shows lying on a sofa with some snacks and cold drinks. But instead this can also be seen as an opportunity to develop skills, learn new things, and invest time on innovative ideas that can later be turned into something big. It is the time to do the things we couldn’t do in daily life schedule. Children can use this time to learn skills like reading and writing that are going to be useful for them throughout their lives. College students can spend their time doing new courses that would give them practical knowledge and enrich their profiles. Elders should invest some time taking care of their health. Yoga and meditation can help them to keep their body and mind calm at the time of pandemic. Before starting anything productive, it is of utmost importance that we keep our physical and mental health stable. It can be done by keeping our body routine same as it was before the lockdown. Apart from that daily exercise or Yoga can be an ideal solution. In the time when everyone has been quarantined, one thing that has helped us to stay connected to the outer world is Technology. Online video platforms like Skype and Duo has helped employees to conduct online meetings. With the help of Zoom application, teachers are able to teach their whole class at once by just sitting at their homes. Online streaming services like Netflix, Prime Video have made this time a little less boring. Social media has helped us to stay in touch with our friends and relatives. Apart from just entertainment and connection, Technology can be used to make this time productive. There are many platforms on the internet that can help us to learn things easily by staying at home. Some things on which we can invest our time during the lockdown are:- Build your Resume: There are many sites on the internet like edX, Coursera, that offer free online courses where learners are just supposed to attend online classes on any interested topic and give small tests at the end of lectures. These courses help in learning new skills that can be later useful and implemented at workplace. Learners also get a certificate at the end of the course which adds value to their resume. Invest time on Startup ideas: Many times we think of starting a new business with an innovative idea but are not able to do it due to lack of clear vision. Now, it is the right time to do researches, contact the right people and go ahead with your plan. Come up with ways of making your idea different from others’. Reading the stories of successful startups can also help you in coming up with some amazing ideas. Learn a new language: If learning a new or a foreign language has been in your bucket list, it is right time to tick it off. With a wide selection of videos and online tutorials on YouTube, you can learn any language. All you need is a phone and internet connection. Learn to play an instrument: There is nothing more stress relieving than playing an instrument. It is believed that everyone should learn playing at least one instrument. With so much free time, it’s never been easier to pick up a new skill. YouTube videos and online tutorials can help you out in this too. Write your Autobiography: Doing this will serve two purposes; you can improve your writing skills and also know what you have done so far in life and what else you want to do. There is no harm in spending some time to relax and chill but it is also important that we come out of this lockdown with some new skills and learning that would help us to be even more productive. Spend some time with family. Stay Home, Stay Safe.
