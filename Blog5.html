Human Centric Lighting at Workplaces

‘You never change things by fighting the existing reality.
To change something, build a new model that makes the existing
model obsolete.’- Buckminster Fuller
With this amazing quote by Mr. Fuller, I would like to tell
about an example that best suits this quote-‘Human Centric Lighting’.
The advanced features of Human Centric Lightings have overshadowed old
lights that had only one feature of giving brightness in a room.
New lights having these features serve multiple purposes at same time.

Lighting is one of the principle elements of this century as it has the potential to influence the health and performance of employees. This decade has been the decade of innovations with new products being introduced that are revolutionizing all modern residential and workplaces. Human Centric Lighting (HCL) System is one of those innovations.
HCL is a type of lighting that helps in improving the physical as well as Emotional health of people. It ensures this by managing the light inside a room with accordance to sunlight throughout the day. Human body is habitual of changing level of light received by Sun around the clock. HCL works on Circadian rhythm to provide appropriate level of light to employees, helping them in increasing productivity and efficiency at work. Circadian rhythm is a natural, internal process that regulates the sleep-wake cycle and repeats roughly every 24 hours.
Apart from increasing productivity and efficiency, HCL has many other benefits. As we know that HCL works on Circadian rhythm, so it helps the employees in getting effective night’s rest. It has a positive impact on sleep pattern of employees which in turn has a positive impact on work performance. Work performance of employees has increased by 18%. Reading speed of people under HCL has also increased up to 30%. HCL works on the concept of working according to daylight. This helps in reducing energy consumption and making the concept highly sustainable.
Some other benefits of HCL include:
Reduced Hyperactivity
Saves money and Environment
Better visual acuity
Reduction in errors and accidents
Improved Moods
Better Morning alertness
The market for Human Centric Lighting is projected to grow from USD 810 Million in 2019 to USD 3655 Million by the end of 2024 with a compound annual growth rate of 32%. The Market share of Europe alone is more than USD 400 Million. More than 40% of the expected growth is expected to come from Europe alone.
Benefits of Human Centric Lighting in different sectors:-
1. Education Sector: – Whether you are a teacher or a student, lighting is factor that affects your accuracy. Human Centered lights in a normal class help not only the student to understand everything but also help teachers to manage class effectively. During tests, these lights can help students to improve their performance as it helps in increasing concentration.

2. Healthcare Sector: – Operation Rooms, Recovery Rooms and Intensive Care rooms are well suited for implementing Human Centric Lightings. It helps in adjusting the lights according to patient’s health. It results in less strain on the eyes, less fatigue and improved health quality. Under these lights, patients fell asleep 30% faster and achieved 8% longer sleep time.

The error rate of workplaces with these lightings has decreased by 45%. 71% of the employees felt more energized while 78% felt happier and healthier. Overall task accuracy has increased by 12% and productivity by 10%. Attrition rate of companies has also decreased

It is important to understand how light can affect a person’s health, efficiency, work performance and overall potential. With new innovations coming in Lighting and Home Automation, creating a safe and healthy home has become easier than ever. They are also energy efficient and easier to control with smart control systems. HCLs help the customers all across the sectors in achieving appropriate balance of lighting. When lighting applications demand both health and performance at the same time, innovations like these have always been fruitful.
