What Millennial think of Home Automation?
It is not really surprising that millennial want smart home technology, but a recent study conducted by Wakefield Research has confirmed it. Their findings show that 86% of millennial are ready to pay more for a home or rental property if it has smart home technology. In fact, millennial are happy to pay an average 20% more in order to secure a home with smart technology. They like electronic access, keyless locks, interconnected doorbells, phone controlled security systems, voice activated assistants, and much more. According to a Neilson global online study, almost 75% of millennial are ready to pay extra for sustainable offerings, and Generation Z is also committed to a positive environmental impact. These young generations of future will demand sustainable offerings in home, and home automation is a simple way to improve energy efficiency and match these future home buyer’s needs. According to The Washington Post, 54% of home buyers say that if they have to choose between two identical houses, one with smart technology, the other without, they would buy the smart home. The post also specified a massive survey of 22,000 home owners done by John Burns Real Estate Consulting which found that not only do potential buyers rank smart technology high when they evaluate housing options; they are also prepared to pay thousands of dollars for it.
